DUMOVA TENNIS
OVERVIEW

What to expect

I started Dumova Tennis because I saw a need in our area: a place where players don’t just “hit balls,” but learn proper mechanics, disciplined footwork, and competitive habits that translate into real match results, whether they’re brand new or working toward Varsity and beyond.

My tennis journey began at age 9, and by 12 I was competing at the national level. I went on to become a nationally ranked junior, compete internationally on the ITF circuit, and reach US Open qualifying. I later played Division I tennis at San Diego State University, served as team captain, and competed at the NCAA level for four years as a scholar-athlete.

Today, I’m proud to be a Sacred Heart Coach for the JV A and 5/6th grade tennis teams. My focus is building a true pipeline of confident, technically sound, match-ready players from Pre-K through 12th grade. The Dumova Tennis pathway is intentionally designed: foundational stroke development → rally tolerance and consistency → point construction and match play → Varsity readiness. For athletes with bigger goals, we also train the skills that matter for USTA competition and high-performance development: footwork patterns, pace tolerance, decision- making under pressure, and strong competitive routines. Parents can expect clear standards, structured progressions, and measurable improvement so players know exactly what they’re working on each week.

For younger players, training is age-appropriate, structured, and fun, using QuickStart-based progressions that build real technique right away: continental/eastern grips, clean swing paths, early preparation, balance, and efficient recovery steps. As players grow, we add the “next level” tools: split-step timing, spacing and contact-point awareness, directional control (crosscourt vs down-the-line), serving and returning fundamentals, smart shot selection, and confidence in points. So by the time tryouts and team placement matter, they’re not “starting tennis”, they’re prepared to compete.

If you’re looking for a program where your daughter will be coached with clarity, standards, and a long-term plan—this is it. Choose her age/level below to reserve a spot for indoor training. Most families see the strongest progress with 2x/week for beginners and 2–3x/week for competitive players. Spots are limited to keep coaching ratios high.

POLICY

Clear expectations: cancellations, make-ups, and refunds.

Cancellation, Make-Up & Refund Policy Acknowledgment

  • A minimum of 24 hours’ notice is required for a cancellation to be eligible for a make-up (including adult clinics, junior clinics, and private lessons).
  • Cancellations made with less than 24 hours’ notice will be charged in full and are not eligible for a make-up.
  • Junior clinic make-ups may be offered when possible but are not guaranteed and are subject to court availability (for example, weekend evenings or holiday breaks).
  • Make-ups must be used within the same indoor season and do not carry over.
  • Refund requests are permitted on or before June 1. Cancellations made after June 1 are non-refundable.
